Watermelon Facts
Watermelon Snow

Watermlon snow is Chlamydomonas nivalis, a species of green algae containing a secondary red carotenoid pigment (astaxanthin) in addition to chlorophyll. Unlike most species of fresh-water algae, it is cryophillic (cold-loving) and thrives in freezing water. Its specific epithet, nivalis, is from Latin and refers to snow.

The Fruit of the Kings

The first recorded watermelon harvest occurred nearly 5,000 years ago in Egypt.
